## Overview

This document outlines the complete Customer Service Manager workflow for R7 Creative, from client handoff through ongoing service management. This workflow is managed in ClickUp and ensures consistent, high-quality client onboarding and service delivery.

### The RaaS Philosophy: Results as a Service

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **We are a Results as a Service (RaaS) partner, not a software vendor.**
>
> Our clients hire us for a specific, measurable outcome. Our process‚Äîa marriage of cutting-edge software, automation, and expert humans-in-the-loop‚Äîis the engine that builds and delivers those results.
>
> **Your role as a CSM is to:**
> - **Drive Collaboration:** Proactively get client inputs (feedback, approvals, assets) needed to keep the engine running without friction
> - **Prove the Result:** Clearly and consistently demonstrate the tangible ROI our engine is producing
> - **Build Momentum:** Integrate our results stream into the client's operation, making us an indispensable part of their growth
>
> You are the client's primary human partner and the lead operator of their results engine.

### What This Document Covers

- Complete client onboarding process (contract to first content published)
- Brand strategy and content planning procedures
- Ongoing service management and bi-weekly sync calls
- Results-focused metrics and performance tracking
- Tools, templates, and procedures for each phase

### Core RaaS Metrics

> [!TIP]
> **Your performance is measured by the results we generate:**
>
> 1. **Time to First Result (TFR)** - Our #1 priority during onboarding
>    - **TFR #1 (Strategic Result):** Brand Pillars delivered within 2 days of Brand Strategy meeting
>    - **TFR #2 (Market-Facing Result):** First content published
>    - **Your Goal:** Be a "concierge" and remove any friction holding up these milestones
>
> 2. **Result Velocity Score (RVS)** - Your data-driven "pulse" on the account
>    - **Client Friction:** Is the client a collaborator or a bottleneck?
>    - **Engine Pace:** Is our internal process running smoothly?
>    - **Result Quality:** What was the outcome of the First Success Review?
>    - **Update After Every Bi-Weekly Sync**
>
> 3. **Client ROI (Return on Investment)** - The ultimate goal
>    - Defined at Discovery Call (defining the "R")
>    - Proven at First Success Review (showing the "I")
>    - Is the measurable return greater than their investment?

## Phase 1: Handoff & Initial Meetings

### Purpose
To officially introduce the service team, align on goals discussed during the sales process, and **create the Results Plan**.

---

### Task: 01 Discovery Call - [DATE]

**Type:** Passoff

**RaaS Play:** **Play 1 - The Handoff & Results Plan**

**Trigger:** Client has agreed to services and is being passed from sales to service team

---

#### Your Goal (RaaS Framework)

**Establish a true partnership and create the Results Plan.**

This is an alignment session to define "results." You are the human face of the engine. You must end this call with the answers to these questions, documented in a **"Results Plan"** (a ClickUp Doc):

**Critical Questions to Answer:**
1. *"What measurable result will make this partnership a 'home run' for you in 90 days?"*
2. *"What is the single most important metric you need our engine to move?"*

---

#### Meeting Objectives

- Introduce the service/implementation team
- Confirm goals and expectations from sales discussions
- **Create the Results Plan document** (ClickUp Doc)
- Set initial timeline expectations and TFR milestones
- Answer any questions about the onboarding process

---

#### Meeting Structure

**1. Partnership Introduction (10 minutes)**
- Introduce yourself as their CSM and primary human partner
- Explain the RaaS philosophy: "We're not just a service provider, we're your results partner"
- Overview of what they can expect from you

**2. Results Definition (15 minutes)**
- Review what was discussed during sales
- Ask the two critical questions above
- Document specific, measurable outcomes they need
- Examples:
  - "More phone calls per week from local searches"
  - "Higher quote acceptance rate"
  - "Reduced time to close deals"
  - "Specific revenue target from new customers"

**3. Timeline & Process (10 minutes)**
- Explain the 4 phases of their journey
- Set expectations for TFR #1 (Brand Pillars in 2 days)
- Set expectations for TFR #2 (First content published)
- Explain First Success Review (2 weeks after first content)
- Introduce bi-weekly sync cadence

**4. Next Steps (5 minutes)**
- Confirm next meeting (Brand Strategy)
- Set collaboration expectations
- Answer questions

---

#### Deliverables

**1. Results Plan (ClickUp Doc)**
Create a new ClickUp Doc in their client folder titled: **"Results Plan - [Client Name]"**

Include:
- **90-Day Home Run Result:** [Their answer to question 1]
- **Primary Metric to Move:** [Their answer to question 2]
- **How We'll Measure Success:** [Specific metrics we'll track]
- **Client's Current State:** [Baseline numbers if available]
- **Target State:** [Where they want to be in 90 days]

**2. Meeting Notes**
- Document in the Discovery Call task
- Reference the Results Plan doc

**3. Updated RVS (Result Velocity Score)**
- Initial assessment of client collaboration readiness
- Document in ClickUp

---

**Next Step:** Begin Phase 2 - Implementation Checklist

## Phase 3: Initial Value Review

### Main Task: First Success Review - [DATE]

**Type:** Value Review

**RaaS Play:** **Play 3 - The "Proof of Result" Review**

**Timing:** 2 weeks after first content is published

**Your Goal:** **Prove the ROI of the initial results delivered.**

**Why This Matters:** This is the most critical meeting for a RaaS partner. This is where you demonstrate the "R" (Results).

---

#### Meeting Preparation

**Before the call, gather:**
1. **The Results Plan** (from Discovery Call)
   - What was their "90-Day Home Run Result"?
   - What metric did they want to move?

2. **Performance Data (First 2 Weeks):**
   - Content published (quantity and platforms)
   - Engagement metrics (views, clicks, interactions)
   - Profile performance improvements
   - Phone calls/inquiries (if trackable)
   - Website clicks from profiles
   - Review generation progress
   - Any customer feedback mentioning online presence

3. **Client's Current RVS Score:**
   - How collaborative have they been?
   - Are they submitting content on time?
   - Quality of captures provided

---

#### Meeting Structure (Use This Script)

**Opening - Reference the Results Plan (5 minutes)**

Use this exact script:

*"In our Discovery Call, you said you needed us to [Measurable Result from Results Plan]. I'm excited to review the first set of results from the [content/media] we published two weeks ago..."*

**Data Review - Show the Numbers (15 minutes)**

1. **Content Delivered:**
   - "We've published [X] pieces of content across [platforms]"
   - Show examples of the content

2. **Initial Performance:**
   - "Here's what we're seeing in the first 2 weeks..."
   - Profile views: [number and % change]
   - Engagement: [likes, comments, shares]
   - Website clicks: [number and % change]
   - Phone calls: [any attribution]

3. **Early Wins:**
   - Highlight any positive feedback
   - Share any customer mentions
   - Note any competitive positioning improvements

**Client Feedback & Calibration (10 minutes)**
- What's working well from their perspective?
- Any concerns or questions?
- Content preferences and adjustments needed
- Are we on track for their "90-Day Home Run Result"?

**Set New Goals for Next Sprint (5 minutes)**
- Based on these initial results, what should we focus on?
- Define metrics for next review period
- Set expectations for acceleration

**Introduce Bi-Weekly Sync Rhythm (5 minutes)**
- Explain the "Results & Strategy" sync calls
- Set recurring calendar invite
- Explain what to expect from each call

---

#### Deliverables

**1. Performance Report**
- Document all metrics reviewed
- Save in client's ClickUp folder
- Email summary to client after call

**2. Updated Results Plan**
- Update with actual baseline numbers
- Adjust targets if needed based on initial results
- Document new goals for next sprint

**3. Updated RVS Score**
- Assess Result Velocity based on:
  - Client collaboration quality
  - Results quality (Positive/Neutral/Negative)
  - Engine performance

## Phase 4: Ongoing Service Management

### Task: Bi-Weekly Sync - [DATE]

**Type:** Sync

**RaaS Play:** **Play 4 - The Strategic Results Sync**

**Frequency:** Every 2 weeks (twice per month)

**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Your Goal:** Build momentum, compound results, and strengthen the partnership.

**IMPORTANT:** These are NOT status updates. They are **"Results & Strategy"** meetings.

**Every Sync Has the Same 3-Item Agenda:**
1. Review new results against goals
2. Calibrate strategy based on data
3. Identify new opportunities to drive more results

**After Every Call:** Update the client's Result Velocity Score (RVS) to track their collaboration and our performance. This helps you spot risks (e.g., slow approvals) before they derail results.

---

### Bi-Weekly Sync Call Structure

#### Before the Call (Preparation)

1. **Review Recent Performance:**
   - Content published since last call
   - Engagement metrics
   - Profile views and clicks
   - Any notable wins or customer feedback
   - Review generation progress

2. **Review Upcoming Content:**
   - Check ClickUp for scheduled content
   - Identify gaps in content calendar
   - Prepare content ideas for discussion

3. **Check Client Communications:**
   - Review any emails or messages from client
   - Note any concerns or requests to address

---

#### During the Call (30 Minutes)

**Follow the 3-Item RaaS Agenda:**

---

**Item 1: Review New Results Against Goals (Minutes 0-10)**

Reference the Results Plan and current goals:
- "Last time we talked, we set a goal to [specific goal]. Here's what we're seeing..."
- Review recent content performance
- Highlight wins and positive trends (tie to their metrics)
- Discuss any engagement patterns
- Share client feedback or customer inquiries
- **Key Question:** "Are we on track for your 90-day home run result?"

---

**Item 2: Calibrate Strategy Based on Data (Minutes 10-15)**

Analyze what's working and adjust:
- "Based on these results, I'm seeing that [observation]..."
- What content types are performing best?
- What topics are resonating with their audience?
- Are there patterns in engagement timing?
- Should we adjust our approach?
- **Key Question:** "What should we do more of? What should we adjust?"

---

**Item 3: Identify New Opportunities & Plan Next Sprint (Minutes 15-28)**

**Content Planning (The Practical Execution):**
- Plan next 6-8 content pieces based on what's working:
  - Content topics/themes (aligned with successful patterns)
  - Content types (video, photo, behind-the-scenes, etc.)
  - Capture dates assigned (specific dates, not vague)
  - Special events or seasonal content
  - Platform priorities
- Assign specific capture dates to client
- Confirm client can capture on assigned dates
- Identify any new opportunities:
  - New platforms to test?
  - New content angles based on customer feedback?
  - Seasonal opportunities coming up?
  - Community events to participate in?

**Document Everything:**
- Document all planning in ClickUp task notes
- Reference the Results Plan if adjusting goals

---

**Wrap-Up: Next Steps (Minutes 28-30)**
- Confirm immediate action items
- Update RVS score assessment (mental note for after call)
- Confirm next sync call date
- Close on a positive, results-focused note

---

#### After the Call (Follow-Up)

1. **Update Result Velocity Score (RVS):**
   - **Client Friction:** Are they collaborative or a bottleneck?
     - Submitting content on time?
     - Providing quality captures?
     - Responsive to requests?
   - **Engine Pace:** Is our internal process running smoothly?
     - Content published on schedule?
     - Production quality maintained?
   - **Result Quality:** Are results positive, neutral, or negative?
     - Moving toward their 90-day goal?
     - Engagement trending up or down?
     - Client satisfaction level?
   - **Document RVS in ClickUp** - This helps spot risks early

2. **Update ClickUp:**
   - Create tasks for all 6-8 planned content pieces
   - Assign capture dates
   - Add any special instructions
   - Tag production team if needed
   - Update Results Plan if goals were adjusted

3. **Send Follow-Up Email:**
   - Recap results discussed and key wins
   - Recap content pieces planned
   - Confirm capture dates
   - Remind client how to submit content (JotForm link)
   - Include any resources or links discussed
   - End with results-focused language: "Excited to keep the momentum going!"

4. **Schedule Next Sync:**
   - If not already scheduled, send calendar invite for 2 weeks out

### Content Planning Best Practices

#### Planning 3 Weeks Ahead
- Always maintain a 3-week content buffer
- Plan 6-8 pieces per call (approximately 3-4 weeks of content at 2-3 posts/week)
- Assign specific capture dates, not just "sometime this week"

#### Content Categories to Rotate
Based on R7 Creative's content framework:

1. **Behind-the-Scenes Work:**
   - Setup processes and preparation
   - Work in progress
   - Problem-solving and diagnosing
   - Building and construction

2. **Customer Success Stories:**
   - Completed projects
   - Before/after transformations
   - Customer testimonials
   - Client interviews

3. **Process Demonstrations:**
   - Step-by-step tutorials
   - Best practices
   - Quality control methods
   - Maintenance procedures

4. **Team Introductions:**
   - Staff spotlights
   - Experience and expertise
   - Behind-the-scenes team culture
   - Values and mission

5. **Tips & Expertise:**
   - Industry advice
   - Preventive maintenance tips
   - Warning signs to watch for
   - Product selection guidance

6. **Equipment & Technology:**
   - New tools and technology
   - Equipment features and benefits
   - Specialized capabilities

7. **Community Involvement:**
   - Local partnerships
   - Volunteer work
   - Donated services
   - Event participation

#### Capture Date Assignment Tips
- Spread capture dates throughout the 2-week period
- Avoid clustering all captures in one week
- Consider client's schedule and busy periods
- Allow buffer time for editing and scheduling
- Plan seasonal/holiday content in advance

## Success Metrics for CSM

### Core RaaS Metrics (Primary Performance Indicators)

**1. Time to First Result (TFR)**
- **TFR #1:** Brand Pillars delivered within 2 days of Brand Strategy meeting
  - Target: 100% on-time delivery
- **TFR #2:** First content published
  - Target: Within 2 weeks of Content Strategy meeting
- Overall time from contract signed to TFR #2
  - Target: < 21 days

**2. Result Velocity Score (RVS) - Track After Every Sync**
- **Client Friction Score (1-5):**
  - 5 = Highly collaborative, submits on time, quality captures
  - 3 = Moderate delays, average quality
  - 1 = Major bottleneck, poor collaboration
- **Engine Pace Score (1-5):**
  - 5 = All content published on schedule, high quality
  - 3 = Occasional delays, acceptable quality
  - 1 = Regular delays, quality issues
- **Result Quality Score (Positive/Neutral/Negative):**
  - Positive = Meeting or exceeding goals, client satisfied
  - Neutral = Some results, client has concerns
  - Negative = Not meeting goals, client dissatisfied

**RVS Aggregate:** Average of scores indicates account health
- **4-5:** Green - Healthy, high-performing account
- **3-3.9:** Yellow - At risk, needs attention
- **< 3:** Red - Critical, immediate intervention needed

**3. Client ROI Achievement**
- Percentage of clients achieving their "90-Day Home Run Result"
  - Target: > 75%
- Measurable metrics moving in positive direction
  - Phone calls, website clicks, inquiries, reviews
- Client retention rate after 90 days
  - Target: > 90%

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### Challenge: Client Not Submitting Content
**Solutions:**
- Use bi-weekly sync to assign very specific capture dates
- Send reminder emails 2 days before capture date
- Offer to do on-site media capture service (paid add-on)
- Simplify capture requests (make them easier/shorter)

### Challenge: Bi-Weekly Sync Calls Rescheduled Frequently
**Solutions:**
- Set recurring calendar invite at client's preferred time
- Send reminder 24 hours before
- Make calls valuable (always share performance wins)
- Keep to 30 minutes (respect their time)

### Challenge: Content Quality from Client is Poor
**Solutions:**
- Provide clearer capture instructions
- Share examples of good captures
- Use Frame IO to give specific feedback
- Offer media capture service as solution

### Challenge: Client Wants to Skip Strategy Meetings
**Solutions:**
- Emphasize importance of Brand Pillars (TTV #1)
- Show value of strategic planning vs. random posting
- Share results from clients who followed process
- Make meetings efficient and valuable
