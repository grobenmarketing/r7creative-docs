# Meeting Agendas

## Overview

This document contains detailed agendas for the three core client meetings in the onboarding and ongoing success process.

---

## 1. Brand Strategy Meeting Agenda

**Meeting Type:** Onboarding
**Duration:** 60-90 minutes
**Timing:** Early in client onboarding, after contract signed and welcome email sent
**Outcome:** Complete brand foundation to inform all content creation

### Pre-Meeting Preparation

**Client To-Do:**
- Complete intake forms
- Review any existing brand materials
- Think about brand differentiators
- Prepare examples of brands they admire

**Team To-Do:**
- Review client intake forms
- Research client's industry and competitors
- Prepare discussion prompts
- Set up ClickUp for note-taking

---

### Agenda

#### Introduction (5 minutes)
- Welcome and meeting overview
- Review objectives and expected outcomes
- Set the stage for collaborative discussion

#### Section 1: Brand Positioning (15-20 minutes)

**Discussion Questions:**
- What makes your business unique?
- How do you want to be perceived in the market?
- What's your competitive advantage?
- Where do you fit in the competitive landscape?

**Deliverable Foundation:**
- Brand Positioning Statement
- Competitive Differentiation
- Market Position

#### Section 2: Unique Value Proposition (10-15 minutes)

**Discussion Questions:**
- What value do you deliver that others don't?
- Why should customers choose you over competitors?
- What's your "unfair advantage"?
- What can you promise that others can't?

**Deliverable Foundation:**
- Core Value Proposition
- Unique Selling Points
- Brand Promise

#### Section 3: Target Market & Customer Avatar (15-20 minutes)

**Discussion Questions:**
- Who is your ideal customer?
- What are their demographics? (age, location, income, etc.)
- What are their psychographics? (values, interests, behaviors)
- What does a day in their life look like?
- Where do they spend their time? (online and offline)

**Deliverable Foundation:**
- Primary Customer Avatar
- Secondary Customer Segments (if applicable)
- Market Demographics
- Customer Psychographics

#### Section 4: Problem & Solution (10-15 minutes)

**Discussion Questions:**
- What problem does your customer face?
- How does this problem impact their life/business?
- How do you solve this problem?
- What does success look like for your customer?
- What transformation do you provide?

**Deliverable Foundation:**
- Problem Statement
- Solution Overview
- Customer Transformation Journey
- Success Metrics

#### Section 5: Messaging Framework (10-15 minutes)

**Discussion Questions:**
- What's your brand voice and tone?
- What key messages should be consistent across all content?
- What words/phrases resonate with your audience?
- What language should we avoid?
- What emotions should your brand evoke?

**Deliverable Foundation:**
- Brand Voice Guidelines
- Core Messaging Pillars
- Key Phrases and Terminology
- Tone and Style Preferences

#### Wrap-Up & Next Steps (5 minutes)

**Review:**
- Summarize key insights
- Confirm brand direction
- Address any remaining questions

**Next Steps:**
- Brand Pillars document delivered within 2 days
- Client review and feedback window
- Schedule Content Strategy Meeting

## 2. Content Strategy Meeting Agenda

**Meeting Type:** Onboarding
**Duration:** 45-60 minutes
**Timing:** After Brand Pillars delivered and reviewed
**Outcome:** Content plan with first 9-12 pieces assigned

### Pre-Meeting Preparation

**Client To-Do:**
- Review and approve Brand Pillars
- Think about content topics of interest
- Prepare examples of content they like
- Consider any upcoming business priorities or campaigns

**Team To-Do:**
- Review approved Brand Pillars
- Research content opportunities in client's industry
- Prepare content theme recommendations
- Prepare content calendar template

---

### Agenda

#### Introduction & Brand Pillars Review (5 minutes)
- Quick recap of Brand Pillars
- Confirm brand foundation is solid
- Transition to content planning

#### Section 1: Content Themes & Topics (15-20 minutes)

**Discussion Questions:**
- What topics are most important to your audience?
- What questions do customers ask most frequently?
- What expertise can you share?
- What content topics support your business goals?
- Are there seasonal or timely topics to consider?

**Output:**
- 3-5 Core Content Themes
- 10-15 Specific Topic Ideas
- Content Topic Priorities

#### Section 2: Content Types & Formats (10-15 minutes)

**Discussion Questions:**
- What content formats work best for your audience?
- What platforms will we publish on?
- What's your comfort level with different formats? (video, written, audio)
- What content types do competitors use successfully?
- What resources/assets do you have available?

**Output:**
- Primary Content Formats
- Platform Strategy
- Production Method (You Capture We Create, We Create, etc.)

#### Section 3: Content Calendar & Frequency (10-15 minutes)

**Discussion Questions:**
- How often should we publish?
- What days/times work best for your audience?
- Are there any important dates or events to plan around?
- What's a sustainable publishing rhythm?

**Output:**
- Publishing Frequency
- Content Calendar Framework
- Key Dates and Milestones

#### Section 4: First Content Batch - Assign 9-12 Pieces (10-15 minutes)

**Activity:**
- Select first 9-12 content topics from discussed themes
- Assign formats to each piece
- Prioritize order of creation
- Identify any that require immediate capture

**Output:**
- First 9-12 Content Pieces Assigned
- Content Brief for Each Piece
- Capture Schedule (if needed)

#### Wrap-Up & Next Steps (5 minutes)

**Review:**
- Confirm content plan
- Address any questions
- Set expectations for content creation process

**Next Steps:**
- Schedule content capture session (if applicable)
- Content production begins
- Client approval process review
- First content publishes by [date]
- First Success Review scheduled for 2 weeks post-publish

## 3. First Success Meeting Agenda

**Meeting Type:** Value Review
**Duration:** 30-45 minutes
**Timing:** 2 weeks after first content published (TTV #2)
**Outcome:** Celebrate wins, review metrics, refine strategy

### Pre-Meeting Preparation

**Client To-Do:**
- Review published content
- Note any feedback or reactions received
- Prepare questions or concerns

**Team To-Do:**
- Compile performance metrics
- Prepare analytics report
- Identify wins and opportunities
- Prepare recommendations for optimization

---

### Agenda

#### Introduction & Celebration (5 minutes)
- Celebrate the milestone of content going live
- Acknowledge the journey so far
- Set positive tone for review

#### Section 1: Content Review (10 minutes)

**Discussion:**
- Review what was published
- Client's reaction to seeing content live
- Any immediate feedback from audience
- Alignment with brand vision

**Questions:**
- How do you feel about the content?
- Does it represent your brand well?
- Any surprises or concerns?

#### Section 2: Performance Metrics (10-15 minutes)

**Present:**
- Reach and impressions
- Engagement metrics (likes, comments, shares)
- Click-through rates (if applicable)
- Video views and watch time
- Audience growth
- Top performing pieces

**Discuss:**
- What's working well?
- What can we learn from the data?
- Are we reaching the right audience?

#### Section 3: Feedback & Optimization (10 minutes)

**Discussion Questions:**
- What content resonated most with you?
- What should we do more of?
- What should we adjust?
- Any new ideas or opportunities?
- Any business developments we should incorporate?

**Output:**
- Feedback for ongoing content
- Strategy adjustments
- New content ideas

#### Section 4: Next Steps & Ongoing Rhythm (5-10 minutes)

**Review:**
- Content pipeline overview
- Upcoming content schedule
- Bi-weekly sync meeting rhythm
- How to provide ongoing feedback
- Approval process reminder

**Establish:**
- Regular meeting schedule (Bi-Weekly Syncs)
- Communication preferences
- Feedback loops
- Expectations for ongoing collaboration

#### Wrap-Up (5 minutes)

**Confirm:**
- Client is satisfied with progress
- Any action items are clear
- Next meeting scheduled
- Client knows how to reach team

## Meeting Best Practices

### General Guidelines

1. **Start and end on time** - Respect client's schedule
2. **Send agenda in advance** - Allow client to prepare
3. **Take detailed notes** - Capture everything in ClickUp
4. **Confirm next steps** - Clear action items before ending
5. **Follow up promptly** - Send summary within 24 hours

### Virtual Meeting Tips

- Test technology before meeting
- Use video when possible for connection
- Share screen for visual collaboration
- Record with client permission for reference
- Use collaborative tools (Miro, Figma, etc.) when helpful

### Note-Taking Protocol

- Designate a note-taker
- Use the ClickUp template for consistency
- Capture exact client quotes when important
- Document decisions and rationale
- Tag action items with owners and due dates

### Meeting Flow

- Keep discussion on track but allow for valuable tangents
- Watch time to cover all agenda items
- Pause for questions regularly
- Summarize key points throughout
- End with clear action items and next steps

---

## Meeting Sequence & Timing

1. **Brand Strategy Meeting** → Early onboarding (Day 3-7)
2. **Content Strategy Meeting** → After Brand Pillars approved (Day 10-14)
3. **First Success Meeting** → 2 weeks after first content publishes (Day 30-45)
4. **Bi-Weekly Syncs** → Ongoing every 2 weeks

This meeting structure ensures thorough onboarding while maintaining momentum and demonstrating value at key milestones.
