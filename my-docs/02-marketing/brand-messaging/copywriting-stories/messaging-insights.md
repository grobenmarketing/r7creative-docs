# Marketing Messaging Key Insights

# What Makes the "John Story" Powerful
*   **Makes the invisible visible** - Most businesses have no idea they're losing opportunities in those 4-minute decision windows
*   **Creates urgency without being pushy** - The time pressure is real and relatable
*   **Shows real-world context** - It's not theoretical; this is exactly how B2B purchasing decisions happen
*   **Demonstrates competitive advantage** - The businesses doing it right win by default
*   **Quantifies the stakes** - "Invisible loss" is a powerful concept
## _The John Story:_
### _Here's What Happened When John Had Exactly 4 Minutes to Find a Local Service Provider_
At 11:56 AM last Tuesday, John sat at his desk when his boss dropped by with an urgent problem. Their company's remote office needed a local service provider immediately. John had exactly four minutes before his lunch break to research options.
What happened next will shock you.
John searched Google and saw five local businesses pop up in the Maps results. All had decent websites. All looked professional. All could solve his problem.
But here's the kicker: John didn't spend time reading about their services or checking their credentials. Instead, he did something completely different.
He looked for signs of life.
John scanned for businesses with recent photos, videos, and active social media. He wanted to know one thing: "Will a real human being answer when I call?"
After exactly four minutes, John closed three of the five browser tabs.
Those three businesses just lost a $10,000 contract and they'll never know it happened.
(John's company needed emergency HVAC repairs for a 15,000 square foot office building. The winning contractor told me later their initial service call turned into a complete system upgrade. Total project value: $11,400.)
**The brutal truth?** This happens 47 times every single day in your local market. Potential customers are eliminating you from consideration before you even know they exist.

## _Outcome Messaging Strengths_
Strong outcome messaging: "more phone calls, emails, quote requests, faster replies, more yeses from existing traffic" - focuses on business results they actually care about
## _Enhancement Opportunities_
**Story Enhancement** - Consider adding a brief follow-up showing what happened to those three closed tabs (John never circled back, or when he did days later, he'd already chosen someone else)
**Invisible Loss Concept** - This could become signature positioning. "How much business are you losing in the 4-minute decision window?" is a powerful hook
**Proof Points** - If you have data on how much more engagement/calls your clients get, that would turbocharge this message
**Cross-Channel Testing** - Test this messaging in different formats (email, video, social posts) to see performance across channels
# Print Shop Channel Partner Messaging Framework
### **The Story (Emotion)**
Print shop hired you as $50K + 20% commission salesperson to reduce vulnerability from losing major accounts. Instead of doing traditional B2B sales, you implemented Google Business + social media strategy. Results: More calls than in 6-8 months, faster quote acceptances, new customers citing social media, landed 47-vehicle fleet deal from Facebook post. They kept your marketing services, never hired replacement salesperson, stayed busy without additional staff.
### **Features**
*   Google Business Profile optimization
*   Review management and customer photo/video collection
*   Social media content creation (3 videos/week)
*   Content shot with phone (behind-scenes, products, company culture)
*   Customer outreach to past clients
### **Benefits**
*   Reduces dependence on large vulnerable accounts
*   Costs less than hiring commissioned salesperson
*   No additional staff needed
*   Leverages existing customer relationships
*   Creates consistent marketing presence
### **Outcomes**
*   More phone calls and inquiries
*   Faster quote responses and acceptances
*   New customers finding you organically
*   Reduced customer acquisition costs
*   Sustainable growth without sales staff overhead
*   Business resilience against account loss
### **Emotional Hook for Print Shops (Channel Partner Angle)**
"What if you could offer your customers a service that makes their print investments work harder for them - while creating a new revenue stream for you and deeper customer relationships?"
### **Channel Partner Value Proposition**
*   Position print shop as full-service marketing partner (not just printer)
*   Create recurring revenue opportunity through referral fees
*   Strengthen customer retention (customers getting better results stick around)
*   Differentiate from competitors who only offer printing
*   Solve customer problems you hear about but can't address
*   Leverage existing trust relationships with local businesses
## **High-Efficiency Channel Partner Acquisition Strategy**
### **Core Challenge**
Limited time (evenings/weekends) + high-value conversations (20 min = 100% clarity) + need to bypass endless email/call cycles
### **Recommended Approaches**
**Direct Visit Strategy**
*   Late afternoon visits (4:30-5:30 PM) when they're winding down
*   "I have 3 minutes to show you something that could add $500-2000/month to your business with zero additional work from you"
*   Bring laptop/tablet with your print shop case study ready to show
**Video Message Breakthrough**
*   Record 2-3 minute personalized video walking through your print shop story
*   Send via text/email: "I did this exact thing at \[Local Print Shop Name\] - 90 seconds to see if this fits your business"
*   Include calendar link for 20-minute call
**Referral Acceleration**
*   Ask current network: "Who do you know that owns a print shop?"
*   Warm introductions convert at much higher rates
*   "Can you text them that I have a 20-minute business idea they should hear?"
**Peak Timing Windows**
*   Tuesday-Thursday, 10-11 AM or 4-5 PM for calls
*   Avoid Mondays (catching up) and Fridays (checking out)
*   Lunch visits can work if you bring food and make it social
**Physical Drop-Off Strategy (When No Phone Number)**
*   Print professional card/flyer with QR code linking to personalized video
*   **Corrected Hook:** "I accidentally discovered how \[Print Shop\] avoided paying a $50K+ salesperson and got better results for under $1K/month"
*   Drop off with front desk: "Can you make sure \[Owner Name\] gets this? It's about a 2-minute cost-saving discovery specific to print shops"
*   Include your contact info and "Watch by \[Date\] - expires Friday" to create urgency
*   Consider small branded item (coffee mug, stress ball) with QR code attached
*   Follow up with email/LinkedIn 2-3 days later: "Did you get a chance to check out that video I dropped off?"
**Refined Card Copy Options:**
*   "I accidentally discovered how \[Local Print Shop\] replaced a $50K salesperson with a $1K/month solution that worked better. 2-minute story: \[QR code\]"
*   "How \[Print Shop Name\] avoided hiring a $50K+ commissioned salesperson and got better results for under $1K/month: \[QR code\]"
## **Partnership Information Flyer - Essential Elements**
**What Print Shop Owners Need to Know at a Glance:**
### **The Partnership Structure**
*   You make warm introduction to their customer
*   Customer watches "John story" video on landing page
*   Customer books discovery call directly with you
*   You handle all service delivery and customer relationship
### **Commission Structure**
*   **Setup Fee:** $300 (50% of $600 implementation fee)
*   **Monthly Recurring:** 10% of monthly plan fee (e.g., $45/month on $450 plan)
*   **Payment:** Monthly, automatic
*   **Duration:** As long as customer stays active
### **Additional Questions They'll Likely Have**
*   **What's required from them?** Just make the introduction - no ongoing work
*   **How do you introduce it?** "I know someone who helps businesses get more calls from their online presence"
*   **What if customer isn't happy?** You handle all customer service/issues
*   **How many customers can they refer?** No limit
*   **Do they need to understand the service?** No - you handle all technical explanations
*   **What types of businesses work best?** Local service businesses (their existing customer base)
*   **How quickly do they get paid?** Within 30 days of customer payment
